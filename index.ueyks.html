Yet another reason wine is good for you

New York Post
Wine drinkers who stop and smell the rosé may be more likely to stave off Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease. A study recently published in Frontiers in Human Neuroscience found that master sommeliers - who have acute senses of smell - also have larger, thicker parts of the brain that are vulnerable to Alzheimer's and Parkinson's than non-wine-experts.
